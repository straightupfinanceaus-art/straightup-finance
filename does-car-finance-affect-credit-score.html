Does Applying for Car Finance Affect My Credit Score?

Published February 4, 2026 · 5 min read

It's a common concern: "If I apply for car finance, will it hurt my credit score?" The answer is: it depends on when and how the enquiry is made.

Let's clear up the confusion around credit checks and car finance.

Soft Enquiries vs Hard Enquiries

Soft Enquiries (Don't Affect Your Score)

A soft enquiry is when someone checks your credit report without it being recorded as an application for credit. These don't affect your score and aren't visible to other lenders.

Examples include:

  • Checking your own credit report
  • Pre-qualification checks by some lenders
  • Background checks by employers

Hard Enquiries (Can Affect Your Score)

A hard enquiry occurs when a lender checks your credit report as part of a formal application for credit. These are recorded on your file and can temporarily lower your score.

Multiple hard enquiries in a short period can signal to lenders that you're desperate for credit, which may hurt your application.

When Does a Credit Check Happen?

Here's the typical process:

  1. Initial quote/pre-approval: Usually NO hard enquiry. Most brokers (including us) can give you a quote without affecting your credit.
  2. Formal application submission: YES, this typically involves a hard enquiry. The lender needs to verify your credit history before approving your loan.

Our Approach

At Straight Up Finance, getting a quote does NOT affect your credit score. We only submit a formal application (which involves a credit check) once you've agreed to proceed, and we'll always let you know before this happens.

How to Protect Your Credit Score

  1. Use a broker — A good broker can compare multiple lenders with just one application, rather than you applying to multiple lenders separately.
  2. Don't "shop around" by applying everywhere — Each application creates a hard enquiry. Multiple enquiries look bad.
  3. Space out applications — If you do apply to multiple lenders, try to do it within a 14-day window. Some scoring models treat this as a single enquiry.
  4. Ask before applying — Before any formal application, ask whether it will involve a credit check.

What If I Have Multiple Enquiries Already?

Don't panic. A few enquiries won't destroy your credit score. The impact is usually small (5-10 points) and temporary. If your credit is otherwise healthy, it shouldn't stop you getting approved.

However, if you have many recent enquiries, it's worth waiting a few months before applying again, or being upfront with your broker about it so they can choose lenders more strategically.

The Bottom Line

Getting a quote or pre-approval typically doesn't affect your credit score. A formal application does create a hard enquiry, but using a broker can minimise the number of enquiries needed.
