What Interest Rate Can I Get on a Car Loan in 2026?

Published February 4, 2026 · 6 min read

One of the first questions people ask when looking for car finance is: "What interest rate will I get?" It's a fair question — the rate you pay can make a significant difference to your total repayments over the life of the loan.

The short answer? Car loan rates in 2026 typically range from about 5.99% to 15%+, depending on your circumstances. But let's break down what actually affects the rate you'll be offered.

Factors That Affect Your Car Loan Rate

1. Your Credit History

This is the big one. Lenders use your credit score to assess how risky you are as a borrower. A strong credit history with no defaults or late payments will get you access to the lowest rates. If you've had some bumps along the way, you may still get approved, but at a higher rate.

2. Your Income and Employment

Stable employment and a consistent income make lenders more comfortable. If you've been in your job for a while and earn enough to comfortably cover repayments, you're in a better position to negotiate.

3. The Age and Type of Vehicle

New cars typically attract lower rates than used cars. And the older the car, the higher the risk for the lender. Most lenders won't finance cars over a certain age (often 10-12 years old at the end of the loan term).

4. Loan Amount and Term

The amount you borrow and the length of your loan can affect your rate. Longer loan terms may have slightly higher rates, and very small loans may not qualify for the best rates.

5. Secured vs Unsecured

Secured car loans (where the car is used as collateral) typically have lower rates than unsecured personal loans used to buy a car.

What Rates Can You Actually Expect?

Typical Car Loan Rates in 2026

  • Excellent credit: 5.99% - 7.99%
  • Good credit: 8% - 10%
  • Average credit: 10% - 13%
  • Poor credit: 13% - 18%+

Keep in mind these are rough guides. Your actual rate will depend on all the factors above, plus the lender you go with and the specific loan product.

How to Get the Best Rate

Here are some practical tips to help you secure a competitive rate:

  1. Check your credit report before applying. Fix any errors and know where you stand.
  2. Save a deposit if you can. Borrowing less reduces the lender's risk.
  3. Don't apply everywhere. Multiple credit applications can hurt your score. Use a broker who can compare options without multiple enquiries.
  4. Compare apples with apples. Look at the comparison rate, not just the advertised rate. The comparison rate includes fees.
  5. Use a broker who doesn't mark up rates. Some brokers add their own margin on top of the lender's rate. We don't.

The Bottom Line

The rate you get on a car loan depends on your individual circumstances. But with the right approach, you can put yourself in the best position to get a competitive deal.
